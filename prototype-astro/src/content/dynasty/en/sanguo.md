achievements:
  economy:
    - title: Military Farming Colonies
      description: Cao Cao established *tuntian* colonies across the north, organising refugees and soldiers to cultivate wasteland. This simultaneously solved the army's food supply and restored post-war agriculture, providing the economic foundation for Wei's dominance.
    - title: Development of Jiangnan
      description: Massive northward-to-southward migration during the Wu period brought advanced farming techniques to the Yangtze delta. Sun Quan's government actively developed paddy agriculture, laying the groundwork for Jiangnan's later emergence as China's economic heartland.
  politics:
    - title: Nine-Rank System
      description: Wei introduced the Nine-Rank Controller system, under which local "Impartial Judges" graded candidates by family background and ability on a nine-tier scale. Initially meritocratic, it later became a tool for aristocratic monopoly of office.
    - title: Governance of Shu Han
      description: Zhuge Liang governed Shu with strict but fair rule of law, clear rewards and punishments, and investment in agriculture and handicrafts. His pragmatic blend of Confucian and Legalist principles kept the small state competitive.
  culture:
    - title: Jian'an Literature
      description: The Jian'an literary school — led by Cao Cao, Cao Pi, Cao Zhi, and the "Seven Masters of Jian'an" — produced poetry of bold, melancholic vigour known as the "Jian'an spirit." Cao Zhi's *Rhapsody of the Goddess of the Luo* is a masterpiece of Chinese literature.
    - title: Hua Tuo and Medicine
      description: Hua Tuo, the era's most famous physician, invented *mafeisan* — a general anaesthetic for surgery — and created the "Five Animals Frolic" exercise regimen. He is regarded as a pioneer of Chinese surgical medicine.

The Three Kingdoms is one of the most romanticised eras in Chinese history. Wei, Shu, and Wu stood in a tripartite balance, producing an extraordinary cast of heroes — Cao Cao's strategic brilliance, Zhuge Liang's selfless devotion, Guan Yu's legendary loyalty. These stories, amplified by the novel *Romance of the Three Kingdoms*, became the most widely known narratives in Chinese culture.

Institutionally, Wei's Nine-Rank system created a new model for official selection, and its military farming colonies restored the war-ravaged economy. Wu's development of the Jiangnan region laid the foundation for the southward shift of China's economic centre. Jian'an literature, with its bold and melancholic spirit, marked an important chapter in Chinese literary history. In 280 the Western Jin conquered Wu and reunified China — but the unity would prove short-lived.
