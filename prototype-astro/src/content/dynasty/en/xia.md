The Xia is traditionally regarded as China's first hereditary dynasty, marking the shift from sage-king abdication to dynastic succession. When Yu's son Qi seized the throne by force, he inaugurated a political model that would endure for four thousand years.

For centuries the Xia existed only in textual tradition, but the Erlitou site in Henan has provided crucial archaeological corroboration. Its palatial foundations, bronze ritual vessels, and urban planning reveal a political entity with the hallmarks of an early state. The Xia lasted roughly seventeen kings and over four centuries before falling to Shang Tang's campaign against the tyrant Jie.
