    - title: Tribal Confederation

The age of Ancient Legends encompasses the mythic narratives of the Three Sovereigns and Five Emperors — the cultural bedrock of Chinese civilisation. The Yellow Emperor unifying the Central Plains, Shennong teaching agriculture, Cangjie inventing writing, Leizu discovering silk: these legends form the collective memory and cultural identity of the Chinese people.

The abdication stories of Yao, Shun, and Yu embody the Confucian ideal of "all under heaven as a public trust." Yu's thirteen-year struggle against the flood remains the quintessential Chinese symbol of perseverance and selfless service. Neolithic sites such as Yangshao, Longshan, and Liangzhu are steadily providing material corroboration for these ancient traditions.
