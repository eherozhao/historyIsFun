achievements:
  economy:
    - title: Bronze Casting Industry
      description: Shang bronze metallurgy reached its apex, producing vessels like the Houmuwu Ding weighing over 800 kilograms. Bronze objects served as ritual vessels, weapons, and symbols of royal authority.
      artifact: Houmuwu Ding
      museum: National Museum of China, Beijing
    - title: Cowrie Currency and Early Commerce
      description: The Shang used cowrie shells as currency and produced bronze imitations. Oracle-bone references to "cowrie" indicate active commodity exchange — the Chinese word for "merchant" (商人) derives from the Shang people's trading tradition.
  politics:
    - title: Inner and Outer Service System
      description: The Shang operated a dual structure — the Inner Service (royal domain under direct rule) and the Outer Service (semi-autonomous allied polities paying tribute and supplying troops). This was the precursor to Zhou feudal enfeoffment.
    - title: Theocratic Rule and Divination
      description: The Shang king served as supreme priest; all major decisions required divination. Oracle-bone inscriptions record sacrifices, campaigns, agriculture, and celestial phenomena — China's earliest systematic written records.
  culture:
    - title: Oracle-Bone Script
      description: Inscriptions on turtle shells and animal bones constitute China's earliest known mature writing system. First identified by Wang Yirong in 1899, over 150,000 oracle bones have been excavated at Yinxu with approximately 1,500 characters deciphered.
      artifact: Yinxu oracle bones
      museum: National Museum of China, Beijing
    - title: Tomb of Fu Hao
      description: Excavated in 1976, Fu Hao's tomb is the only undisturbed royal burial at Yinxu. It yielded 468 bronzes and 755 jades, providing irreplaceable evidence for Shang royal life and the status of women.
      artifact: Fu Hao owl-shaped zun vessel
      museum: National Museum of China, Beijing

The Shang is the first Chinese dynasty attested by contemporary written records. After Pangeng moved the capital to Yin, the dynasty remained at Anyang for over 270 years. The more than 150,000 oracle-bone inscriptions unearthed at Yinxu transformed Shang history from legend into documented fact.

Shang bronze casting reached heights unmatched in the ancient world. The king ruled as both sovereign and supreme priest, consulting spirits through divination before every major decision. At its zenith under Wu Ding, the dynasty's military reach extended in all directions, and his consort Fu Hao became the first woman in Chinese written history to command armies. King Zhou's tyranny provoked a coalition led by King Wu of Zhou; the Shang fell at Muye, but its writing, bronze technology, and ritual traditions were inherited wholesale by the Zhou.
