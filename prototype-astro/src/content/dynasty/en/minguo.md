The founding of the Republic of China marked the end of over two thousand years of imperial rule and the dawn of the republican era. The Xinhai Revolution, the May Fourth Movement, and the New Culture Movement drove profound political and intellectual transformation, embedding the modern ideals of democracy, science, and vernacular expression in the national consciousness.

Yet the Republican period was also one of China's most turbulent. Warlord fragmentation, Japanese invasion, and civil war between the Nationalists and Communists brought immense suffering. Victory in the War of Resistance against Japan was China's first fully successful modern war against foreign aggression, but the ensuing civil war ended with the Nationalist retreat to Taiwan and the beginning of a new chapter on the mainland.
