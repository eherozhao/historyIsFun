The Qing was China's last imperial dynasty and one of its most territorially expansive. During the Kangxi-Qianlong golden age the empire was powerful, the population surged, and the economy led the world. Cao Xueqin's *Dream of the Red Chamber* stands as the crowning achievement of Chinese classical literature, and the *Complete Library of the Four Treasuries* was a cultural project of unprecedented scale.

Yet beneath the splendour, crisis was brewing. Isolation from the Industrial Revolution left China unprepared; the humiliating defeat in the Opium War opened the floodgates of foreign encroachment. The Taiping Rebellion, the Self-Strengthening Movement, the Hundred Days' Reform, and the Boxer catastrophe followed in rapid succession as the dynasty lurched toward its end. In 1912 the last emperor abdicated, bringing over two thousand years of imperial rule to a close.
