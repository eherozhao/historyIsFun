    - title: The Grand Canal
      description: Stretching roughly 2,700 kilometres and linking five river systems, the Grand Canal enabled grain and goods from the Jiangnan to reach the north efficiently. It is the world's longest artificial waterway, still in use today, and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2014.
    - title: Equal-Field and Rent-Service-Cloth System
      description: The Sui refined the Northern Dynasties' equal-field system and paired it with the rent-service-cloth tax (grain, labour, and textile). This created a coherent fiscal framework that underpinned Sui-Tang economic prosperity.
  politics:

      description: Emperor Wen abolished the Nine-Rank system and began selecting officials by examination; Emperor Yang established the *jinshi* degree. The examination system broke the aristocratic monopoly on office and endured for 1,300 years until the late Qing.
    - title: Three Departments and Six Ministries
      description: The Sui formalised the central government into three departments (Secretariat, Chancellery, Department of State Affairs) and six ministries (Personnel, Revenue, Rites, War, Justice, Works) — separating policy drafting, review, and execution. Though it lasted only thirty-seven years, the Sui was a pivotal dynasty bridging the centuries of division and the Tang golden age. Emperor Wen ended nearly three hundred years of north-south partition, created the imperial examination system, formalised the Three Departments and Six Ministries, and refined the equal-field system — institutional innovations that became the bedrock of Tang prosperity.

Emperor Yang's Grand Canal was even more consequential: this north-south waterway served as China's economic lifeline for over a millennium. But his simultaneous pursuit of the canal, the eastern capital, and three failed Korean campaigns exhausted the people and ignited rebellion. The Sui fell in 618, yet its institutional legacy was inherited almost wholesale by the Tang — a parallel to the Qin-Han succession seven centuries earlier.
