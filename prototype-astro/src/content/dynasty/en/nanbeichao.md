The Northern and Southern Dynasties was the era of deepest ethnic fusion in Chinese history. In the north, Xianbei and other nomadic peoples who had established kingdoms gradually Sinicised — Emperor Xiaowen's reforms marked the climax of this process. In the south, the Han cultural tradition continued and the economy flourished. The two halves faced each other for nearly three centuries before the Sui finally reunified China.

The cultural achievements of this period were extraordinary. The Yungang and Longmen grottoes represent the summit of Buddhist art; Zu Chongzhi's calculation of pi led the world for a millennium; Liu Xie's *Literary Mind and the Carving of Dragons* laid the theoretical foundations of Chinese literary criticism. The ethnic fusion and cultural exchange of the Northern and Southern Dynasties prepared the ground for the splendour of the Sui and Tang.
