      description: On Dong Zhongshu's recommendation, Emperor Wu "dismissed the hundred schools and honoured Confucianism alone," making it the state ideology.     - title: Recommendation System
      description: The Han established a system whereby local officials recommended talented individuals to the central government under categories such as "filial and incorrupt" and "flourishing talent." The Western Han was China's first long-lived unified empire. After Liu Bang founded the dynasty from humble origins, decades of recuperative governance brought the state to its zenith under Emperor Wu. Wu drove back the Xiongnu, opened the Silk Road, and established Confucian orthodoxy — making the Han one of the most powerful empires in the contemporary world.

The dynasty's cultural achievements were equally brilliant. Sima Qian's *Shiji* invented the biographical-annalistic form that every subsequent dynastic history would follow, and the Mawangdui tombs revealed the richness of Han material culture. In its later years, however, consort-clan dominance grew unchecked until Wang Mang usurped the throne and founded the short-lived Xin Dynasty, whose impractical archaising reforms collapsed within fifteen years.
