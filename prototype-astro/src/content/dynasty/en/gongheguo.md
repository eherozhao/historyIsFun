The founding of the People's Republic of China in 1949 opened a new chapter in Chinese history. Starting from an impoverished agrarian base, New China built an independent industrial system; achievements such as the "Two Bombs, One Satellite" programme established its standing as a major power.

Since Reform and Opening Up in 1978, China has achieved remarkable economic growth, rising from poverty to become the world's second-largest economy and lifting hundreds of millions out of destitution. Breakthroughs in high-speed rail, crewed spaceflight, and quantum communication demonstrate China's growing technological strength. The Chinese nation is on the path toward great rejuvenation.
