The Ming was the last unified dynasty founded by the Han Chinese, lasting 276 years. Zhu Yuanzhang rose from destitution to build an empire, abolishing the chancellorship and pushing imperial autocracy to its extreme. Under the Yongle Emperor the dynasty reached its zenith — the Forbidden City, Zheng He's voyages, and the Yongle Encyclopedia all testified to Ming ambition and power.

The mid-to-late Ming saw an unprecedented commercial boom as silver poured in from abroad and proto-capitalist production emerged. Wang Yangming's School of Mind, Li Shizhen's *Compendium of Materia Medica*, and three of the Four Great Classical Novels were products of this era. Yet eunuch dominance, factional strife, and fiscal crisis deepened until the dynasty fell to the twin blows of peasant rebellion and Manchu invasion.
