The Eastern Han was re-established by Liu Xiu after the collapse of Wang Mang's Xin Dynasty, with its capital at Luoyang. The Guangwu Restoration brought stability and recovery; Cai Lun's improvement of papermaking, Ban Chao's thirty-year command of the Western Regions, and the formal introduction of Buddhism were among the era's landmark achievements.

From the mid-dynasty onward, however, the Eastern Han fell into a destructive cycle of consort-clan and eunuch dominance. The Partisan Proscriptions devastated the scholar-official class, the Yellow Turban Rebellion shook the foundations of rule, and regional warlords rose to fill the vacuum. In 220 Cao Pi deposed the last Han emperor, and China entered the age of the Three Kingdoms.
