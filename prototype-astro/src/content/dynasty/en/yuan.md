  economy:
    - title: Postal Relay System
      description: The Yuan established over 1,500 relay stations (*zhanchi*) across the empire, ensuring rapid communication and facilitating trade. This network promoted economic exchange on a national scale.
    - title: Paper Currency
      description: The Yuan was the world's first government to use paper money as its primary currency, issuing the *Zhongtong* and *Zhiyuan* notes. Late-dynasty overprinting caused severe inflation — a contributing factor in the dynasty's collapse.
  politics:
    - title: Provincial System
      description: The Yuan divided the empire into the Central Secretariat's direct domain and ten Branch Secretariats (provinces), each headed by centrally appointed officials. This system is the direct ancestor of China's modern provincial divisions.
    - title: Four-Class Hierarchy
      description: Yuan subjects were ranked into four tiers — Mongols, *Semu* (Central Asians), Han Chinese (northerners), and Southerners — with unequal legal and career rights. This ethnic discrimination intensified social tensions and undermined the dynasty's stability.

The Yuan was the most territorially expansive dynasty in Chinese history and the first unified regime founded by a northern nomadic people. Kublai Khan's provincial system laid the foundation for China's modern administrative geography; the relay-station network connected every corner of the empire; and paper currency circulated nationwide. The dynasty's openness brought East-West exchange to unprecedented levels — Marco Polo's account revealed the wealth of the East to Europe.

Culturally, Yuan drama ranks alongside Tang poetry and Song *ci* as one of the three summits of Chinese literature, and blue-and-white porcelain matured into an art form exported across the world. Yet the four-class ethnic hierarchy and late-dynasty corruption inflamed social tensions, and the Red Turban Rebellion ultimately toppled Mongol rule.
