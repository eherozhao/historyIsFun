The Southern Song held the south for over 150 years. Though it lost the northern half of China, it reached the pinnacle of economic and cultural achievement in Chinese feudal society. The Maritime Silk Road made it one of the wealthiest polities in the contemporary world, and the widespread use of paper currency marked the height of commercial sophistication.

Culturally, Zhu Xi's Neo-Confucian synthesis shaped Chinese thought for centuries, while the patriotic verse of Xin Qiji and Lu You still stirs the heart. Yet the political rift between war and peace factions plagued the court throughout, and Yue Fei's unjust execution remains one of history's great tragedies. At the Battle of Yamen in 1279, the last Southern Song resistance was destroyed — China fell entirely under nomadic conquest for the first time.
